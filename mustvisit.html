Here are some of the places you must visit in Banglore

1. Ulsoor Lake

Ulsoor Lake is one of the biggest lakes in the country and a must-visit place in Bangalore, India. There are lots of islands on the lake and it is the ideal place to go boating. You can visit the islands via a boat cruise, booked from the boat club. Ulsoor Lake is also surrounded by a lot of ancient temples, including the Halasuru Someshwara Temple, the oldest temple in the area. The Ganesha Festival is also celebrated here, every year, in August or September, depending on the cycle of the moon. The Ganesha Festival honors the elephant-god, Lord Ganesha, who promises to bring good fortune.

Ulsoor Lake
Address: Near MG Road, Bangalore, Karnataka
Opening Hours: Th-T 6am to 8pm
Price: Free

2. Nandi Hills

Nandi Hills is 60 km (37 miles) from Bangalore and is a must-visit place for trekking. This former fortress and summer retreat residence of Tipu Sultan has now become a place to escape the city. Tipu Sultan’s Summer Palace, also known as Tashk-e-jannat, or the “envy of heaven” features intricately carved arches and painted walls and ceilings and is a popular spot for visitors. Careful climbing up towards Tipu’s Drop. It’s safely barricaded but it’s still 600-meter (1,968.5-foot) drop from the top. If you’ve included this place on your itinerary, make sure to allot a full day, as it’ll take around two hours to get here and two hours to go back to Bangalore.

Nandi Hills
Address: Close to NH207, towards Chik Ballapur
Opening Hours: 8:30am to 5:30pm
Price: Indians 5 INR/0.07 USD Foreigners 100 INR/1.40 USD


3. Wonderla Amusement Park

Wonderla Amusement Park ranks second best in a ranking of the top amusement parks in India and seventh overall in Asia, which makes it one of the must-visit places in Bangalore, India. With 60+ rides, there is something for everybody in your group. No wonder it’s called the Amazement Park! For those who want to enjoy attractions without heart-stopping moments, try the Adventures of Chikku, Cinemagic 3D, or the Musical Fountain and Laser Show.
For the adult adventurers, try the Hang Glider and Wonder Splash. If you have kids, check out the Pirate Ship, Net Walk and Crazy Cars. And these are just the rides on land! There are also water rides and high-in-the-sky thrill rides. If you want to enjoy all the rides, Bangalore Park is near the Bangalore Resort, so you can be at the Park as it opens and stay until it closes. With so many attractions the park has to offer, you’ll need at least 2-3 full days to enjoy the everything.

Wonderla Amusement Park
Address: 28th km, Mysore Road, Bangalore
Opening Hours: Normal Season - Weekdays: 11am to 6pm Normal Season - Weekend/Holidays: 11am to 7pm Peak Season - 11am to 7pm
Price: Peak Season Adult: 1400 INR/19.58 USD Child: 1130 INR/15.80 USD

4. Bangalore Palace



Built in 1878 and influenced by Tudor and Scottish Gothic architecture, Bangalore Palace is still one of the most popular places in the region. Bangalore Palace and its grounds occupy around 45,000 square feet (4,180.6 square meters) and visitors are greeted by Roman arches at the entrance. The vine-covered walls of the palace make you feel as if you’re in the English countryside and the palace interiors boast Hindu decor and are home to numerous paintings, including those from India’s most famous painters. It’s also home to a collection of photographs that show the evolution of Bangalore. To fully enjoy the palace, be sure to set aside two to three hours to explore the grounds.
Bangalore Palace
Address :Palace Road, Vasanth Nagar, Bangalore
Opening Hours: 10am to 5:30pm
Price: 230 INR/3.24 USD (Indians), 460 INR/6.47 USD (Foreigners)

5.Jawaharlal Nehru Planetarium

The Jawaharlal Nehru Planetarium is a project of the Bangalore Association for Science Education (BASE), who is devoted to making learning science fun and interactive. They offer shows, science exhibits, monthly movie screenings, the Jawaharlal Nehru Planetarium Club, workshops and activities on National Science Day. If you have a child or a nephew or niece who loves to learn about science, check out their summer programs, which range in length from one to 15 days.
Jawaharlal Nehru Planetarium
Address: Bangalore Association for Science Education, Sri T. Chowdaiah Road, High Grounds, Bangalore
Opening Hours: T-Sun 10:15am to 5:15pm
Price: 50 INR/0.70 USD (Adults) 30 INR/0.42 USD (Students/Children up to 16 years)
